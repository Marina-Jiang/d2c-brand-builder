## 1. Influencer Evaluation Scorecard

When selecting influencers, score them across these dimensions (1-5 scale):

| Criterion | What to look for | Weight by Tier |
|-----------|-----------------|----------------|
| **Audience Fit** | Demographics match target persona (age, gender, location, interests) | High for all tiers |
| **Engagement Rate** | Comments, saves, shares relative to follower count. Look for genuine engagement, not bot activity | Critical for Micro/Nano |
| **Content Quality** | Production value, storytelling ability, visual consistency | High for Macro/Micro |
| **Brand Alignment** | Do their values and aesthetic match the brand? Would the partnership feel natural? | High for all tiers |
| **Audience Authenticity** | Follower growth pattern, engagement patterns, audience location vs. claimed location | Mandatory check for all |
| **Platform Performance** | Which platforms drive the best results for their content type? | Varies by campaign goal |
| **Previous Brand Work** | Have they done successful brand partnerships? In similar categories? | Nice-to-have |
| **Responsiveness** | How quickly and professionally do they communicate? | Practical consideration |

### Red Flags
- Sudden follower spikes without corresponding content virality
- Engagement rate that's unusually high or low for their follower count
- Comments that are generic or emoji-only (bot indicators)
- Audience concentrated in countries that don't match the target market
- History of promoting competing brands simultaneously

### Benchmark Engagement Rates (approximate, varies by platform)
- Instagram: Nano 3-8%, Micro 2-5%, Macro 1-3%
- TikTok: Nano 5-15%, Micro 3-8%, Macro 2-5%
- YouTube: All tiers 2-8% (varies widely by niche)

## 2. Outreach Templates

### Cold Outreach — Nano/Micro Influencer (Product Seeding)

Subject: Love your content — collab with [Brand]?

Hi [Name],

I'm [Your Name] from [Brand]. We make [brief product description] and I've been following your content on [platform] — especially loved your [specific post/video reference].

We think you'd be a great fit for our brand and would love to send you [product] to try. No strings attached — if you love it and want to share with your followers, that would be amazing.

Interested? I can share more details!

[Sign-off]

### Partnership Inquiry — Micro/Macro Influencer (Paid Collaboration)

Subject: Paid partnership opportunity — [Brand] x [Influencer Name]

Hi [Name],

I'm reaching out from [Brand], a [brief positioning statement]. We're launching [campaign/product] and are looking for creators who [reason they were selected — be specific].

We'd love to discuss a paid collaboration that could include:
- [Deliverable 1, e.g., 1 Instagram Reel + 2 Stories]
- [Deliverable 2, e.g., 1 YouTube integration]

We're flexible on creative direction — your authentic voice is what matters most to us.

Would you be open to a quick chat? Happy to share our brand deck and discuss budget.

[Sign-off]

## 3. Collaboration Models & Compensation

### Model Types

**Product Seeding / Gifting**
- Best for: Nano influencers, initial testing
- How it works: Send free product, no payment, no obligation to post
- Typical response rate: 20-40% will post organically
- Cost: Product cost + shipping only

**Flat Fee**
- Best for: Micro and Macro influencers, guaranteed deliverables
- How it works: Negotiate a fixed payment for specific deliverables
- Typical ranges (US market, very approximate):
  - Nano (10K-50K): $100-$500 per post
  - Micro (50K-500K): $500-$5,000 per post
  - Macro (500K-5M): $5,000-$50,000+ per post
- Rates vary enormously by niche, platform, and engagement quality

**Affiliate / Commission**
- Best for: Nano influencers, performance-focused campaigns
- How it works: Unique discount codes or affiliate links, influencer earns % of sales
- Typical commission: 10-20% of attributed sales
- Pro: Aligns incentives. Con: Influencers may prefer guaranteed payment

**Hybrid (Flat Fee + Commission)**
- Best for: Micro influencers, ongoing partnerships
- How it works: Lower flat fee plus commission on sales
- Good balance of guaranteed income for influencer and performance incentive for brand

**Ambassador Programs**
- Best for: Proven top performers across all tiers
- How it works: Long-term exclusive or semi-exclusive partnership
- Typically includes: Monthly product, monthly retainer, commission, early access, co-creation opportunities

## 4. Content Brief Template

When briefing influencers, provide:

**Brand Overview** (keep it short — 2-3 sentences)
- What the brand is, who it's for, what makes it different

**Campaign Goal**
- Awareness / Consideration / Conversion — be specific about what success looks like

**Key Messages** (provide 2-3 max, not a list of 10)
- What should the audience take away from the content?

**Product Details**
- Which product(s) to feature
- Key features to highlight
- Any specific use cases or styling suggestions

**Content Requirements**
- Platform and format (e.g., TikTok video 30-60 seconds)
- Must-include elements (product demo, unboxing, discount code mention, etc.)
- Hashtags and tags
- Disclosure requirements (FTC compliance for US, ASA for UK, etc.)

**Creative Freedom**
- Be explicit about what they CAN'T do (competitor mentions, certain claims)
- Be generous about what they CAN do — prescriptive briefs produce inauthentic content

**Timeline**
- Draft submission deadline
- Revision window
- Publishing date/window

**Compensation Details**
- Payment terms and timeline
- Usage rights (can the brand repurpose the content? For how long? On which channels?)

## 5. Performance Tracking

### Metrics by Campaign Goal

**Awareness Campaigns**
- Impressions / Reach
- Video views (3-second, 15-second, completion rate)
- Brand mention volume and sentiment
- New follower growth on brand accounts
- Cost per thousand impressions (CPM)

**Consideration Campaigns**
- Engagement rate (likes, comments, saves, shares)
- Link clicks / Swipe-ups
- Website traffic from influencer content
- Time on site from influencer traffic
- Cost per engagement (CPE)

**Conversion Campaigns**
- Attributed sales (via discount codes or affiliate links)
- Revenue per influencer
- Return on influencer spend (ROIS)
- Customer acquisition cost (CAC) via influencer channel
- New vs. returning customer split

### Attribution Methods
- **Unique discount codes**: Most reliable for direct attribution
- **UTM parameters**: Track traffic source in Google Analytics
- **Affiliate links**: Automated tracking and commission calculation
- **Post-purchase surveys**: "How did you hear about us?" captures untrackable word-of-mouth
- **Promo code + UTM combo**: Best practice — use both together

## 6. Contract Essentials

Key terms to include in influencer agreements:

- **Deliverables**: Exact number, type, platform, format
- **Timeline**: Draft submission, revision rounds, publish date
- **Compensation**: Amount, payment schedule, payment method
- **Usage rights**: Can the brand repost? Use in ads? For how long?
- **Exclusivity**: Can they work with competitors during/after the campaign?
- **Content approval**: Does the brand review before publishing?
- **FTC/Disclosure compliance**: Mandatory — the brand is liable if the influencer doesn't disclose
- **Cancellation terms**: What happens if either party needs to back out?
- **Performance guarantees**: Some brands include minimum view/engagement thresholds with makeup deliverables

Always consult local legal requirements — disclosure rules vary significantly by country.
